Faculty-Driven. University-Wide.

The 1%… the 9%…
and the 90%

Every major university is racing to respond to AI. Most are doing it the same way — investing in the 1% building new models and the 9% designing transformation strategy. But the real AI innovation won't happen in the lab or the strategy deck. It will happen in the 90% — and this faculty-driven, university-wide initiative is built to enable it.

1%

The Builders

PhD researchers and engineering teams building new AI models inside research labs. Most universities start and stop here.

9%

The Strategists

Business schools studying digital transformation, AI strategy, and organizational change. Important — but not enough.

90%

This Is Where Innovation Happens

The newsroom. The editing suite. The hospital. The architecture firm. The policy office. The classroom. The startup. This is where the real AI innovation will happen — and where most universities are leaving people behind. This is our focus.

Our Structural Advantage

Massively Parallel
Innovation

We are not centralizing AI innovation in one department and asking everyone to come to us. We are distributing it — across every school, every discipline, every domain — through a shared academic infrastructure that any faculty member can plug into and build on.

This is what makes this initiative different from anything else happening in higher education. Not one program. Not one school. Every school, simultaneously, building AI curriculum that is native to their own discipline.

The Shared Foundation

TAC foundational courses provide the infrastructure every school builds on — no prerequisites, no coding required, designed for any discipline.

Domain-Specific Innovation

Each school builds AI curriculum native to their discipline. CTPR 499 at the School of Cinematic Arts is the first. Every school in this initiative has the same opportunity.

University-Wide Impact

Innovation happens simultaneously across schools — not sequentially inside one program. This is the model that scales.
